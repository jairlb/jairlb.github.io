Hi, My name is Jair!

My name is Jair (it is phonetically pronounced "Yaw + ear"). In 2019 I attained a B.S in Cognitive Science specializing in Human Computer Interaction from UCSD. Since the time that I started pursuing my undergraduate degree at university, I worked part-time at a tech startup as a junior software engineer, where I have been able to expand my software engineering skills and be a part of an agile development process of software creation. I have since transitioned to a full time position at the same company upon gradution where I continue to work in software, front-end, and mobile development.
I am always trying to learn more about my cross-sectional field of interests and find the point where they all meet, and they do! As I am very passionate about how using the knowledge of cognitive science can expand my knowledge of computer science and vice versa.

A Holistic Approach to Technology

I love cognitive science. In hindsight, it is an almost embarrassing fact that I knew nothing about Cognitive Science when I transferred to UCSD to join the Cognitive Science undergraduate program in 2017. Fast-forward to now, I have come to realize that the field involves everything I want from the sciences and the skillset for working in technology that I knew I wanted, which was to not only have a theoretical and abstract background of how software and technology work, but also a more nuanced human appraoch, to inform the design of systems ultimately used my humans.
My cognitive science background provides me with a more holistic and versatile view of technology and thinking. The psychology and neuroscientific components of cognitive science provide an essential foundation to inform my design-based specialization and has instilled a user-first mindset and the ability to conduct user research to inform UI/UX creation and upkeep. Meanwhile my computer science and programming knowledge provides me with more technical skills in addition to my experience working at a startup living the weekly Agile 2.0 life and making sure my tickets pass through Q/A and peer review lanes before moving to done. :)

Applying Knowledge to the Life Sciences

Throughout my time pursuing my undergraduate at UCSD and presently, I have gained experience working in the life sciences through working at at a local startup that offers technological solutions and assistance for digital clinical trials. I have been able to see the medical field rapidly evolve to embracing more streamlined approaches that utilize the technology of today.
I am fortunate to have this experience as it has demonstrated to me the synthesis between abstract technology, software engineering, and product applications while allowing me to build skills working in a formal development team. I have been able to witness and be a part of software creation from planning, launch, and upkeep which has reinforced how positively influential abstract sciences can be in the medical field and general life sciences.
My experience, along with my interdisciplinary and mixed cognitive science and computer science background, has me excited and eager to continue growing my skills and being apart of this kind of progress in the future.
