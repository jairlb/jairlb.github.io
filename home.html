Hi, My name is Jair!

My name is Jair (it is phonetically pronounced "Yaw + ear") and I have recently graduated from UCSD with a B.S in Cognitive Science specializing in Human Computer Interaction (in ~T-7 days!). For the last two years I have spent this time working part-time at a tech startup as a junior software engineer, where I have also been able to expand my software engineering skills and be a part of an agile development process of software creation.
I am always trying to continue to learn more about my cross-sectional field of interests and find the point where they all meet, and they do! As I am very passionate about how essential using the knowledge of cognitive science is to expand my knowledge of computer science and vice versa.

A Holistic Approach to Technology

I love cognitive science. In hindsight, it is almost an embarrassing fact that I knew nothing about Cognitive Science when I transferred to UCSD to join the Cognitive Science undergraduate program in 2017. Fast-forward to now, I have come to realize that the field involves everything I want from the sciences and the skillset for working in technology that I knew I wanted, which was to not only have a theoretical and abstract background of how software and technology work, but also a more nuanced human aspect, to inform the design of systems ultimately used my humans.
The advantages I have with my CogSci background is that it provides me to have a more holistic and versatile view of technology and thinking. The psychology and neuroscientific background inform my design-based specialization and had instilled a user-first mindset, in addition to the ability to conduct user research to inform UI/UX creation and upkeep. Meanwhile my programming knowledge provides me with more technical skills in addition to my experience working at a startup living the weekly Agile 2.0 life and making sure my tickets pass through Q/A and peer review lanes before moving to done. :)

Applying Knowledge and the Life Sciences

Throughout my two years pursuing my undergraduate at UCSD and presently, I have gained experience working in the life sciences through working at at a local startup that offers technological solutions and assistance for clinical trials in 1-3 phases.
I am fortunate to have this experience as it has demonstrated to me the synthesis between abstract technology, software engineering, and product applications while allowing me to build skills as a working in a formal team. I have been able to witness and be a part of software creation from planning to launch, which has reinforced to me the influential effects that abstract sciences can have in the medical field and life sciences.
My experience, along with my interdisciplinary and mixed cognitive science and computer science background, has me excited and eager to continue growing my skills and being apart of this kind of progress in the future.
